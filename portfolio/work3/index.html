@ me next time: a sentiment analysis of tweets at Twin Cities mayors

In my data science final project, I worked in a group that looked at perceptions of Minneapolis mayor Jacob Frey and St. Paul Mayor Melvin Carter on twitter and in the comments section of the local newspaper.

We focused in on the viral spread of an incident where a Christmas tree was decorated with racist memorabilia in Minneapolis’ 4th police precinct, and how it’s viral spread impacted online perceptions of both mayors. I compared tweets directed at Minneapolis mayor Jacob Frey and St. Paul mayor Melvin Carter. The news quickly moved across the country and globe with people tweeting about the tree and mentioning Frey from Minneapolis to the Bronx to Iraq. Carter, on the other hand, has had a relatively quiet few weeks online. We performed sentiment analysis, Tf-Idf, and other exploratory analysis to better understand how citizens viewed, commented, and tweeted about their mayors.
