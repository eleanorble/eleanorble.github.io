Mac-Grove equity and inclusivity task force

I’ve worked for MGCC for about a year and a half. Recently, I’ve moved from general event support to helping research, plan, and implement our neighborhood-wide and city council approved equity plan.

The Macalester-Groveland Community Council (MGCC) functions as a community sounding board and advisory agent to the city. It listens, inquires, plans and acts on a wide range of matters, including zoning and land use, traffic and parking, and capital spending. It is also is a vehicle for area residents, businesses and institutions to identify, plan, and address community goals.

MGCC is addressing equity in it’s work engaging citizens in the planning process. I am helping organize and lead the Inclusivity Task Force, a group creating and implementing a neighborhood-wide equitable civic-engagement initiative. In recognition that MGCC could do more in pursuit of equitable engagement, the Inclusivity Task Force (ITF) was created to directly address these questions:

What voices are missing? What are the barriers to participation? become involved? What would happen if our work included these missing voices?
