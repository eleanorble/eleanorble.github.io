Mac-Grove equity and inclusivity task force

I’ve worked for MGCC for about a year and a half. Recently, I’ve moved from general event support to helping research, plan, and implement our neighborhood-wide and city council approved equity plan. The Macalester-Groveland Community Council (MGCC) functions as a community sounding board and advisory agent to the city. It listens, inquires, plans and acts on a wide range of matters, including zoning and land use, traffic and parking, and capital spending.

URBAN GIS: NORTHSIDE ACHIEVEMENT ZONE

In the spring of 2017 I took an Urban GIS course that partnered with the Northside Achievement Zone (NAZ). I worked in the Housing Stability group and focused on gentrification susceptibility, evictions, and shifting homeownership patterns.

@ me next time: a sentiment analysis of tweets at Twin Cities mayors

In my data science final project, I worked in a group that looked at perceptions of Minneapolis mayor Jacob Frey and St. Paul Mayor Melvin Carter on twitter and in the comments section of the local newspaper.

Evictions in Minneapolis - a mixed methods study

I’m a quantitative lead in a large-scale study of the causes and implications of disproportionate evictions in North Minneapolis using tax records, rental license data, and 100 landlord and tenant interviews. This amazing team, led by Dr. Brittany Lewis at the Center for Urban and Regional Affairs, has worked with a youth participatory action research team, testified at state legislative hearings, advised on a Minnesota state Supreme Court eviction case, and frequently presents research updates to an Evictions Advisory Council.

Who owns land in Hennepin county? A quantitative approach

I am currently working on a research project about shifting forms of ownership and owner location in Hennepin county over the past 15 years.
